# German Theme And Color Translation Rules

Use this note when the German task covers theme titles, palette names, dual-tone descriptions, accessibility labels, or custom color import/export UI.

## Theme Titles

- Write theme titles as natural German product names, not as stiff word-for-word calques.
- German compounds are allowed when they read naturally, but avoid forced abstract compounds that feel machine-translated.
- Prefer short title rhythm suitable for a settings picker.
- Avoid adding articles in list headings or preset-family labels unless the phrase clearly requires one.

## Dual-Tone Descriptions

- Keep dual-tone descriptions short and color-led.
- Prefer compact color-list phrasing such as `Farbe A und Farbe B.` or `Farbe A, Farbe B und Farbe C.`
- Do not turn the description line into lyrical atmosphere prose, lore text, or marketing copy.
- Avoid repeating `Dual-Tone-Palette`, `Look`, or other framing words when the UI already makes the theme context obvious.

## Accessibility Labels

- Prefer a clean pattern such as `Theme „Name“: Farbe ...`
- Do not build awkward compounds like `X-Design` or `X-Theme-Design`.
- Keep the color reading explicit and scannable for screen-reader output.

## Material Palette Names

- Use established German color names when they sound natural in a palette picker.
- Keep palette labels compact and product-like.
- Do not over-translate into unusual poetic wording just to avoid loanwords.
- Mixed loanwords are acceptable when they are standard German color vocabulary, but avoid random switching between plain noun labels and overly literal phrases.

## Product-Term Consistency

- Keep `Dual-Tone` as a stable product term with consistent capitalization and hyphenation in German UI text.
- Keep `Material` unchanged as the design-system family name.
