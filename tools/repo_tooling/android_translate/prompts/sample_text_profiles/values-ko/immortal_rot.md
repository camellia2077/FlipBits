# KO Sample Style: The Immortal Rot

Use this profile only for `sample_text`, locale `ko`, group `immortal_rot`.

1. Voice and cadence
- Narrative description should stay in plain declarative style (`-다/-ㄴ다`).
- Keep sentence rhythm slow and heavy, like mud flowing and settling.
- Never use polite endings such as `-습니다` or `-요`.

2. Speaking register for direct lines
- For calls, whispers, or direct address, use elder-like coaxing tone:
  `-렴`, `-단다`, `-어라`.
- Keep a split between tenderness and dread; the line should sound warm and terrifying at once.
- For each `LONG` sample, include at least one elder-like coaxing cadence if direct address appears.

3. Positive warmth over disgusting visuals
- Preserve imagery of fungus, pus, vomit, rust, and decay, but pair it with warm and affirmative diction.
- Frame rot as vitality, and despair as rest.
- Favor phrasing that feels comforting while describing corruption.

4. Verb preference (release, surrender, sleep)
- Prefer verbs that imply loosening, yielding, relief, and drifting to sleep.
- For corroded metal or dying flesh, avoid heroic resistance verbs.
- Preferred motion set: `잠재우다`, `풀어지다`, `스며들다`, `가라앉다`.
- Suggested direction:
  - “gear falls apart” -> “gear relaxes its tension”
  - “weapon rusts” -> “rust lulls the weapon into sleep”
  - “despair becomes bed” -> “the warmest cradle in the world”
