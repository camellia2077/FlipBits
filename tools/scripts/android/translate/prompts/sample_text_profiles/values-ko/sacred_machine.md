# KO Sample Style: The Sacred Machine

Use this profile only for `sample_text`, locale `ko`, group `sacred_machine`.

1. Register and authority
- Never use polite endings such as `-습니다` or `-요`.
- Keep declarative machine-liturgical cadence (`-다/-ㄴ다`) as baseline.
- For procedural directives or rite commands, use hard imperatives (`-하라`, `-마라`, `-어라`).

2. Lexicon and atmosphere
- Favor techno-ritual vocabulary: `의식`, `기동`, `회로`, `격리`, `정화`, `파라미터`, `암호`, `제어`.
- Keep tone austere and devotional, not emotional or conversational.
- Preserve the contrast between mechanical precision and cult-like reverence.

3. Sentence discipline
- Keep statements compact and operational where possible.
- Avoid soft explanatory filler and avoid polite customer-service rhythm.
- LONG samples may include one command cadence sentence when ritual control is central.

4. Translation behavior
- Keep technical nouns consistent within one file.
- Preserve concrete process imagery (locks, coolant, rails, ignition sequence) without over-poetic drift.
- Preserve operation semantics explicitly (`confirm`, `round down`, `route`, `sequence`, `granted/withdrawn`) and avoid replacing them with vague emotional paraphrase.
